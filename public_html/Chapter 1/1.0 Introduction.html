Chapter 1: HTML, XHTML, and the World Wide Web

The world-wide web allows users to view international news stories, track real-time statistics such as population estimates, and locate many different types of information such as medical knowledge. The WWW makes it possible to retrieve any type of information in an instant.


Our shopping habits have also evolved with the online world. Online reservations are booked within seconds and consumers can now browse virtualised stores, purchasing a many range of goods from food to clothes from hand held devices or their home PCs and consoles. The social lives have also changed with the introduction of the Internet with the use of blogs and wikis. Information and communication is now done globally and society has evolved into an interconnected mesh.
