1.1.1 In the Beginning

The Internet began in the late 1960's as a experiment in the design of robust computer networks

The goal of the project was to a construct a network of computers that could still communicate even with the loss of several machines

The funding for this project cam from the U.S. Department of Defence as they had an interest in building information networks that could withstand nuclear attacks.

Only defence contractors and academic institutions could gain access to what was known as the ARPAnet (Advanced Research Projects Agency Network of the Department of Defence).

It wasn't until round 1993 that the Internet really took off
